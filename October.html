DRAFT 10.10.18 Reflection on DrawBots and possible future directions

I considered my DrawBots having had a few weeks break from them to prepare for presentations on our work, possible future directions, and a question that we have for our first seminar of the year. I began with the DrawBots on the floor of my studio surrounded by the paint drops from other works I have made which is where they belong to me - integrated into the things I love.

Even though the circuits of the DrawBots are not something I have ever previously considered as being handmade or requiring some dexterity and skill, making these fairly complex and compact circuits taught me that technology and maybe especially art technology is not necessarily machine-made even if it looks like it or engages with a presentation that it is “futuristic”. It requires as much coordination and experience to solder a circuit as draw a room in perspective.

It also was useful to reflect on the fact that people engaged with my work and automatically humanised the robots frequently. I had people more than once call them synonyms of cute and a couple even likened them to being my (robo)babies. I even have my personal name for these robots - RoboBallz and the more serious name DrawBots for public consumption. I wonder if it is because they (unintentionally) look quite handmade/as having a human touch potentially because they have been painted rather than have lights on or being clearly made from metal/plastic and are therefore less clearly identified as technology. There is a kind of aesthetic of technology or of the future which is quite bullshit to some extent, at least when it comes to physical objects. My robots were made with my hand, but it would have been very easy to make them look like technology. To make them shiny, or light up. And things that are screen based automatically have an appearance forced on them to some extent no matter what is being portrayed on the screen. It is limited to pixels ultimately.

Working with them I found very interesting as they were supposed to be something that worked, rather than I worked with. However, I ended up playing with them and exploring them like I would any material. I called them by their (Photon) names. I saw different personalities in them, even though the personality was actually the characteristics of the specific motors or how many revolutions the motor had turned, for example. They actually became quite an unusual thing to work with as an artist because they were more interactive than my paper or my favourite pens. Rather than being an external system, as with any system of making art, the artist can never be erased. Ten years ago I took this fairly seriously and it was a highly productive and creative pursuit to try and get to the bottom of the eternal contradiction - the artist will always come up with the initial idea even if every other idea after that is out of their control. I treat it now as a constructive game and with these robots I actually more clearly saw that I am part of it rather than external to the system. If you could reduce making art down to a button, the artist would be in the button.

DRAFT 15.10.18 Lisa Nakamura reading

Perhaps also some thoughts on directions for my work I have been considering the work I want to make this year further. I am going to start with the obvious one which is to develop the code that runs the DrawBots further so they start gaining more independence from me. Plus learning how to do state machines will be useful anyway.

I am also interested in sound, drawing and machines which has led my thinking to vibrations rather than trying to make something musical. Looking at old machines for playing music and considering how a CD player or record player could be broken down and reconstructed differently led me down the route of inventions and failed inventions briefly. The idea of chains of vibrations, or taking vibrations from the wind over Arctic ice sheets, or taking my drawings, perhaps some of my highly intricate drawings and making sound from them could be interesting. Machinery for reading earthquakes is also something I have been looking at a little.

Consider also the research I did into robots and the idea of the robot this week. I think it was for last week.

DRAFT 22.10.18 Roboballz directions and Line Follower

This week I discussed possible areas of research and continued work to develop the complexity of the roboballz. I now have a functioning, basic state machine with a button to turn off and on the Photon whilst remaining in a constant reverse state.

I have also been going to a free class for women to learn HTML etc which I quite like in some ways and learning about the internet is slightly crazy. It would be good to bring this into my work if I can. It is very weird going into an accountancy firm’s offices to do it though - I am a “girl” when I go in (it’s run by Girls First), blending into a bunch of “girls”, not dressed like I used to dress, not like the suits around me even though to me it’s so familiar it’s comfortable (and yet not what I want).

I am also still wanting to make my disruptive economics website and sell art work for £1 or something.
