The Alien

This work explores how to interact with a computer to create work. It explores the physical differences between myself and the computer. The speed at which the computer operates far outstrips my own abilities. My first experiments in this were frustrating. What also emerged was the vast difference in the visual output. The computer was not only faster, it looked different. It's aesthetics are different. Straight lines, light, perfect accuracy. I felt self conscious and uncomfortable in the first three experiments working with a computer below. A computer has no touch, and if it does, it's touch is a flickering loop.

Imitation of Life

These works are a development of my initial steps in working with a computer to create art. They are necessary steps to consider the visual language that would exist if I was to work compatibly with a computer to make art given that we are so different as to be aliens. The works are exploring in more detail the differences between my hand and the computer hand. And considering the visual implication of the different hands and, by extension, why it is the computer hand looks the way it does.

These works present further exploration of the differences between myself and a computer. Or the machinery that represents computers. I am exploring how I feel about interacting with it in this form as much as how it could look aesthetically.
